About

I am an Assistant Professor in Mathematical Education in the School of Mathematical Sciences at South China Normal University, Guangzhou, China.

My research focuses on mathematical competitions, mathematical identity, gifted education, and the growth and development of outstanding mathematicians.

My current work examines the educational and career development of mathematically gifted students, with particular attention to their learning experiences, motivation, identity development, and long-term growth.

Research

Broadly speaking, I am interested in mathematics education, gifted education, mathematical identity, and the long-term development of mathematically talented students.

My recent research focuses on students participating in mathematical competitions and examines how educational experience, motivation, identity, and social environments shape their subsequent academic and career development.
